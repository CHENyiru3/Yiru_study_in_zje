    S2.W3.2 Structural study of epigenetic regulation in transcription

    组蛋白修饰和染色质调控的结构生物学：实验工作流程

    一、样品制备和蛋白质纯化¶

    1. 重组蛋白表达
    2. 将编码SAGA复合物组分(如Gcn5、Ada2、Ada3)的基因克隆到表达载体中
    3. 转化到大肠杆菌表达菌株(BL21(DE3))
    4. 使用IPTG诱导蛋白表达
    5. 收集细胞并在天然条件下制备裂解液
    6. 使用亲和层析(His标签、GST标签)纯化蛋白

    7. 通过离子交换和分子筛层析进一步纯化

    8. 核小体组装

    9. 表达并纯化重组组蛋白(H2A、H2B、H3、H4)
    10. 从纯化的组蛋白中重折叠组蛋白八聚体
    11. 制备含有核小体定位序列的DNA片段
    12. 通过盐梯度透析重构核小体

    13. 用天然PAGE验证组装

    14. 引入组蛋白修饰

    15. 化学合成修饰的组蛋白
    16. 使用纯化的HATs/HDACs进行酶促修饰
    17. 使用天然化学连接法整合修饰的组蛋白尾部

    18. 通过质谱和Western blot验证修饰

    19. 复合物形成

    20. 将纯化的SAGA复合物与修饰的核小体结合
    21. 优化缓冲条件以形成稳定复合物
    22. 通过凝胶过滤层析验证复合物形成
    23. 通过分析型超速离心评估化学计量比

    二、结构分析¶

    1. 冷冻电镜样品制备和数据收集
    2. 将3-4μl纯化的复合物应用于放电处理的电镜网格上
    3. 吸除多余液体并在液态乙烷中快速冷冻
    4. 筛选网格以评估冰质量和颗粒分布
    5. 在电子显微镜(如Titan Krios)上收集高分辨率数据
    6. 使用直接电子探测器记录电影

    7. 使用运动校正软件处理数据

    8. 冷冻电镜数据处理

    9. 进行CTF估计和校正
    10. 使用自动和手动拾取选择颗粒
    11. 提取颗粒并进行2D分类
    12. 生成初始3D模型
    13. 通过迭代分类优化3D结构
    14. 应用后处理以提高分辨率

    15. 使用傅里叶壳相关验证分辨率

    16. X射线晶体学(针对特定结构域)

    17. 筛选单个结构域的结晶条件
    18. 优化晶体生长参数
    19. 收获晶体并准备衍射
    20. 在同步加速器设施收集衍射数据
    21. 处理衍射数据并进行相位确定
    22. 构建和优化原子模型

    23. 验证模型质量

    24. 模型构建和优化

    25. 将原子模型拟合到冷冻电镜密度图中
    26. 根据实验数据优化模型
    27. 验证模型几何形状和与密度的拟合
    28. 分析蛋白质-蛋白质和蛋白质-核小体界面

    Structural Biology of Histone Modifications and Chromatin Regulation: Experimental Workflow

    I. Sample Preparation and Protein Purification¶

    1. Recombinant Protein Expression
    2. Clone genes encoding SAGA complex components (e.g., Gcn5, Ada2, Ada3) into expression vectors
    3. Transform into E. coli expression strains (BL21(DE3))
    4. Induce protein expression with IPTG
    5. Harvest cells and prepare lysates under native conditions
    6. Purify proteins using affinity chromatography (His-tag, GST-tag)

    7. Further purify via ion exchange and size exclusion chromatography

    8. Nucleosome Assembly

    9. Express and purify recombinant histone proteins (H2A, H2B, H3, H4)
    10. Refold histone octamers from purified histones
    11. Prepare DNA fragments containing nucleosome positioning sequences
    12. Reconstitute nucleosomes by salt gradient dialysis

    13. Verify assembly by native PAGE

    14. Introduction of Histone Modifications

    15. Chemical synthesis of modified histones
    16. Enzymatic modification using purified HATs/HDACs
    17. Native chemical ligation to incorporate modified histone tails

    18. Verify modifications by mass spectrometry and Western blotting

    19. Complex Formation

    20. Combine purified SAGA complex with modified nucleosomes
    21. Optimize buffer conditions for stable complex formation
    22. Verify complex formation by gel filtration chromatography
    23. Assess stoichiometry by analytical ultracentrifugation

    II. Structural Analysis¶

    1. Cryo-EM Sample Preparation and Data Collection
    2. Apply 3-4μl of purified complex to glow-discharged EM grids
    3. Blot excess liquid and plunge-freeze in liquid ethane
    4. Screen grids for ice quality and particle distribution
    5. Collect high-resolution data on microscope (e.g., Titan Krios)
    6. Record movies with direct electron detector

    7. Process data using motion correction software

    8. Cryo-EM Data Processing

    9. Perform CTF estimation and correction
    10. Select particles using automated and manual picking
    11. Extract particles and perform 2D classification
    12. Generate initial 3D model
    13. Refine 3D structure through iterative classification
    14. Apply post-processing for resolution enhancement

    15. Validate resolution using Fourier shell correlation

    16. X-ray Crystallography (for Specific Domains)

    17. Screen crystallization conditions for individual domains
    18. Optimize crystal growth parameters
    19. Harvest crystals and prepare for diffraction
    20. Collect diffraction data at synchrotron facility
    21. Process diffraction data and perform phase determination
    22. Build and refine atomic models

    23. Validate model quality

    24. Model Building and Refinement

    25. Fit atomic models into cryo-EM density maps
    26. Refine models against experimental data
    27. Validate model geometry and fit to density
    28. Analyze protein-protein and protein-nucleosome interfaces

    III. Biochemical and Functional Characterization¶

    1. In Vitro HAT/HDAC Activity Assays
    2. Prepare substrates (nucleosomes with unmodified histones)
    3. Incubate with purified SAGA complex or HDACs
    4. Detect acetylation/deacetylation by:
      • Radiolabeled acetyl-CoA incorporation
      • Western blotting with modification-specific antibodies
      • Mass spectrometry analysis

    5. Determine enzyme kinetics (Km, Vmax)

    6. Binding Affinity Measurements

    7. Isothermal titration calorimetry (ITC) to measure thermodynamics
    8. Surface plasmon resonance (SPR) to measure binding kinetics
    9. Fluorescence anisotropy for smaller domain interactions

    10. Compare binding affinities for modified vs. unmodified substrates

    11. Mutational Analysis

    12. Design mutations at key interaction interfaces
    13. Generate mutant proteins using site-directed mutagenesis
    14. Purify mutant proteins and assess structural integrity
    15. Measure effects on binding and enzymatic activity
    16. Correlate with structural observations

    IV. Cellular and Genomic Studies¶

    1. Cell Line Engineering
    2. Generate yeast strains with mutations in SAGA components
    3. Create human cell lines with CRISPR-Cas9 edited HATs/HDACs
    4. Develop cell lines expressing histone mutants (e.g., K→R to prevent acetylation)

    5. Verify modifications by Western blotting and mass spectrometry

    6. Chromatin Immunoprecipitation (ChIP)

    7. Crosslink proteins to DNA in living cells
    8. Sonicate chromatin to appropriate fragment size
    9. Immunoprecipitate with antibodies against:
      • Modified histones (e.g., H3K27ac)
      • SAGA components
      • Transcription factors
    10. Purify and analyze DNA by qPCR or sequencing (ChIP-seq)

    11. Map genome-wide distribution of modifications and binding

    12. Transcriptome Analysis

    13. Extract RNA from wild-type and mutant cells
    14. Prepare RNA-seq libraries
    15. Sequence and analyze differential gene expression

    16. Correlate with ChIP-seq data to link histone modifications to gene regulation

    17. HDAC Inhibitor Studies

    18. Treat cells with selective HDAC inhibitors
    19. Monitor changes in histone modification patterns
    20. Analyze effects on transcription factor binding
    21. Assess impact on gene expression

    V. Data Integration and Analysis¶

    1. Structural Analysis and Visualization
    2. Generate molecular models using PyMOL or Chimera
    3. Create electron density maps showing key interaction interfaces
    4. Identify critical residues involved in substrate recognition

    5. Analyze conformational changes upon substrate binding

    6. Computational Analysis

    7. Perform molecular dynamics simulations
    8. Calculate electrostatic surface potentials
    9. Model energetics of binding interactions

    10. Predict effects of mutations or inhibitors

    11. Statistical Analysis

    12. Apply appropriate statistical tests for biochemical data
    13. Calculate significance of ChIP-seq and RNA-seq results
    14. Perform correlation analysis between structural features and functional outcomes

    15. Validate reproducibility across biological replicates

    16. Data Presentation and Interpretation

    17. Create structural figures showing key interfaces
    18. Generate graphs comparing enzymatic activities
    19. Develop genome browser tracks for ChIP-seq data
    20. Integrate findings into mechanistic models of transcriptional regulation

    This comprehensive experimental workflow enables researchers to understand the structural basis of histone modifications and their role in transcriptional regulation, providing insights into fundamental epigenetic mechanisms and potential therapeutic targets.

    1. Experimental Purpose:¶

    • Scientific Question: The study aims to understand how histone modifications (e.g., acetylation, methylation) and chromatin structure regulate transcription. Specifically, it investigates the structural mechanisms by which histone modifications influence the binding of transcription machinery and the regulation of gene expression.
    • Experimental Design: The study uses structural biology techniques (e.g., cryo-EM, X-ray crystallography) to determine the molecular structures of key epigenetic regulators, such as histone acetyltransferases (HATs) and histone deacetylases (HDACs), and their interactions with nucleosomes and transcription factors.

    2. Model System:¶

    • Model System: The study primarily uses yeast and human cells, as these systems are well-characterized and provide a direct link to eukaryotic transcription regulation. The study focuses on the SAGA complex, a well-known HAT complex involved in transcription regulation.
    • Why This System?: Yeast and human cells are appropriate for this study because they allow for the manipulation of specific histone modifications and chromatin structures. The SAGA complex is relevant because it plays a key role in regulating transcription through histone acetylation.
    • Alternative Model Systems: Other model systems like bacteria or plants could be used, but they lack the complexity of eukaryotic chromatin and histone modifications.
    • Ethical Considerations: Using human cell lines requires ethical approval, especially when dealing with patient-derived samples. The study likely adheres to ethical guidelines for genomic research.

    3. Measurement Approach:¶

    • Techniques: The study employs several techniques:
      • Cryo-electron microscopy (cryo-EM): To determine the structures of epigenetic regulators (e.g., SAGA complex) and their interactions with nucleosomes.
      • X-ray crystallography: To obtain high-resolution structures of specific histone modifications and their interactions with transcription factors.
      • Biochemical assays: To study the functional roles of histone modifications in transcription regulation.
      • Chromatin immunoprecipitation (ChIP): To study the binding of transcription factors to histone-modified chromatin.
    • Why These Methods?: Cryo-EM and X-ray crystallography were chosen because they allow for the visualization of molecular structures at atomic resolution, which is crucial for understanding the mechanisms of epigenetic regulation. Biochemical assays and ChIP were used to study the functional roles of histone modifications in transcription.
    • Technical Replicates: The study likely uses multiple replicates (e.g., n=3 or more) to ensure the robustness of the structural data.
    • Potential Biases: Biases could arise from sample preparation or data interpretation. These are minimized by using standardized protocols and cross-validation with biochemical assays.

    4. Group Setting:¶

    • Experimental Groups: The study compares several groups:
      • Wild-type cells vs. cells with specific histone modifications: To study the effects of histone modifications on transcription.
      • Cells with intact SAGA complex vs. cells with SAGA complex mutations: To study the role of the SAGA complex in transcription regulation.
      • Cells treated with HDAC inhibitors vs. untreated cells: To study the effects of histone deacetylation on transcription.
    • Negative Controls: Cells without specific histone modifications or with intact SAGA complex serve as negative controls to study the effects of histone modifications and SAGA complex mutations.
    • Biological Replicates: Multiple samples are used in each group (e.g., n=3 or more) to ensure reproducibility.
    • Modified Group Design: A dose-response experiment could be added to study how varying levels of histone modifications or HDAC inhibitors affect transcription.

    5. Data Analysis & Presentation:¶

    • Data Processing: Raw structural data from cryo-EM and X-ray crystallography are processed to generate 3D models of the molecular structures. Biochemical and ChIP data are analyzed to study the functional roles of histone modifications in transcription.
    • Statistical Tests: The study uses statistical validation methods (e.g., cross-validation, resolution assessment) to ensure the accuracy of the structural models. Differential binding analysis is performed to identify key transcription factors regulated by histone modifications.
    • Data Presentation: Results are presented using 3D structural models, graphs, and tables, which are clear and free of misleading scaling. For example, the study uses electron density maps to show the structures of histone modifications and bar graphs to compare transcription factor binding.
    • Confirmation: The structural findings are confirmed using biochemical assays and functional studies, which validate the proposed mechanisms of epigenetic regulation.

    General Observations:¶

    • The study provides a detailed understanding of how histone modifications and chromatin structure regulate transcription through the SAGA complex and other epigenetic regulators.
    • The findings highlight the importance of histone acetylation and deacetylation in regulating gene expression and suggest potential therapeutic targets for diseases linked to epigenetic dysregulation.
    • The results have significant implications for understanding the molecular basis of epigenetic regulation and developing therapies for cancer and other diseases.
